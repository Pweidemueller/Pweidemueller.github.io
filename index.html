About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Scholar in Nicola Müller’s group at the Experimental and Population-based Pathogen Investigation Center (EPPIcenter) at UCSF.

My research focuses on investigating local transmission dynamics of respiratory pathogens using phylodynamic modelling paired with diverse data inputs. I aim to inform public health interventions that are tailored to specific transmission dynamics on a local level.

Previously, I completed my PhD in Evangelia Petsalaki’s group at EMBL-EBI, studying context-dependency of cancer vulnerabilities. In general, I am passionate about Bayesian statistics and building mathematical models that bridge complex biological data with actionable insights to improve pandemic preparedness and response to infectious disease outbreaks.

Research Interests

Phylodynamic Modelling

Investigating local transmission dynamics of respiratory pathogens by combining genomic data with mathematical models.

Bayesian Statistics

Applying Bayesian frameworks to model specific properties of different data modalities and infer evolutionary parameters.

Public Health

Developing effective, data-driven public health interventions tailored to local transmission dynamics.
